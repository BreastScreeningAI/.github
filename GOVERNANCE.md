# Governance

BreastScreening-AI repositories are maintained to support project communication,
research prototypes, documentation, and collaboration around AI-assisted breast
screening.

BreastScreening-AI is the spinoff initiative under SensiPerception. It sits
alongside related research initiatives for internal proof-of-concept work,
doctoral and postdoctoral research, and international academic collaboration.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers should:

- Keep public project information accurate and up to date.
- Review contributions for clarity, safety, privacy, and technical fit.
- Avoid publishing patient-identifiable data, credentials, private datasets, or
  confidential clinical material.
- Make clinical and AI-related claims carefully, with appropriate limits.
- Preserve repository-specific instructions when they differ from organization
  defaults.

## Contribution Decisions

Contributions are evaluated based on:

- Relevance to the BreastScreening-AI project.
- Evidence, reproducibility, and documentation quality.
- Privacy and security impact.
- Maintainability and reviewability.
- Alignment with the research or communication purpose of the target repository.

## Ecosystem Movement

Some work may originate in MIMBCD-UI as an internal proof of concept, move into
MIDA when it becomes part of a broader medical imaging diagnosis assistant
research line, and later be adapted for BreastScreening or BreastScreening-AI
when the collaboration or spinoff context requires it.

When moving or forking work between initiatives, maintainers should check:

- Whether the target initiative matches the maturity and collaboration context.
- Whether authorship, supervision, and student contribution history are clear.
- Whether sensitive data, private datasets, or credentials are excluded.
- Whether clinical claims are still accurate in the new context.
- Whether repository names, links, licenses, and documentation need to change.

## Clinical And Research Claims

Project materials should distinguish research, prototype, communication, and
validated clinical use. Any claim about clinical performance, deployment, or
patient impact should be backed by appropriate evidence and review.
